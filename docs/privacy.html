Privacy Policy

Effective July 5, 2026

Verdancy is a plant identification and care app. This policy explains, in plain language, what we collect and why. We collect the minimum needed to run the app, we don't sell your data, and we don't show ads.

What we collect

  • Account: when you sign in with Apple we receive a unique account identifier and (if you share it) your email address. This is how your garden syncs across reinstalls and devices.
  • Plant photos: photos you choose to save are stored in a private cloud bucket accessible only to your account. Photos you submit for identification or diagnosis are processed and are not stored by our servers.
  • Plant data: your saved plants, care schedules, care history, milestones, and tree tally.
  • Purchases: subscription status is processed by Apple and RevenueCat. We never see your payment details.

AI processing

When you scan a plant, the photo is sent to Google's Gemini model via our server to produce an identification or diagnosis. The photo is forwarded for that single request and immediately discarded by our server — it is not used to train models by us, and it is not written to our storage unless you explicitly save the plant.

What we don't do

  • No selling or renting of personal data.
  • No third-party advertising or tracking SDKs.
  • No location tracking.

Notifications

Care reminders are scheduled locally on your device. We run no push-notification infrastructure.

Deleting your account

Settings → Account → Delete account permanently removes your account, your plants, your photos, and your sign-in identity from our systems, immediately.

Service providers

Amazon Web Services (hosting, storage, sign-in), Google (Gemini AI processing of submitted photos), Apple (sign-in, payments), RevenueCat (subscription management).
